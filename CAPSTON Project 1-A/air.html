Air Pollution

Air pollution occurs when harmful substances are introduced into Earth's atmosphere. These pollutants can be in the form of solid particles, liquid droplets, or gases, and can come from natural sources or human activities.

Major Causes

  • Vehicle emissions from cars, trucks, and motorcycles
  • Industrial factories and power plants burning fossil fuels
  • Agricultural activities releasing ammonia and methane
  • Burning of waste and fossil fuels for heating
  • Construction and mining activities releasing dust

Health Effects

  • Respiratory diseases like asthma and bronchitis
  • Cardiovascular problems and heart disease
  • Reduced lung function and lung cancer
  • Premature death in severe cases
  • Aggravation of existing health conditions

Air Quality Index

Good: 0-50

Moderate: 51-100

Unhealthy: 101-150

Environmental Impact

Air pollution contributes to climate change by increasing greenhouse gases in the atmosphere. It causes acid rain that damages forests and aquatic ecosystems. Pollutants can reduce visibility and harm wildlife habitats.

What You Can Do

  • Use public transportation or carpool when possible
  • Reduce energy consumption at home and work
  • Support clean energy initiatives
  • Plant trees to improve air quality
  • Avoid burning waste materials

Alarming Fact

Air pollution causes approximately 7 million premature deaths worldwide each year, making it one of the world's largest environmental health risks.
