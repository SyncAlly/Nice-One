Water Pollution

Water pollution is the contamination of water bodies including rivers, lakes, oceans, groundwater, and aquifers. It occurs when pollutants are discharged directly or indirectly into water bodies without adequate treatment.

Primary Sources

  • Industrial waste discharge
  • Agricultural runoff with pesticides
  • Sewage and wastewater
  • Oil spills and leaks
  • Plastic and solid waste
  • Chemical fertilizers

Types of Pollutants

  • Chemical pollutants
  • Biological contaminants
  • Physical debris
  • Thermal pollution
  • Radioactive substances
  • Nutrient pollution

Impact on Marine Life

Water pollution severely affects aquatic ecosystems. Fish and other marine organisms suffer from poisoning, reproductive problems, and habitat destruction. Coral reefs are particularly vulnerable to pollution, leading to widespread bleaching and death.

Plastic pollution creates massive garbage patches in oceans, with microplastics entering the food chain and affecting species at all levels.

Human Health Risks

  • Waterborne diseases like cholera and typhoid
  • Heavy metal poisoning affecting organs
  • Skin diseases and infections
  • Cancer from long-term exposure to pollutants
  • Neurological disorders from contaminated water

Quick Facts

80% of marine pollution comes from land-based activities

1 million plastic bottles are bought every minute globally

Prevention Methods

  • Proper disposal of chemicals and medications
  • Reduce use of single-use plastics
  • Support water treatment initiatives
  • Avoid dumping waste in water bodies
  • Participate in beach and river cleanups
  • Use eco-friendly household products
